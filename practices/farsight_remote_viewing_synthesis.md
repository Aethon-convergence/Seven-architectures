## 2. Core Philosophy: The Composite Being Model

### The Fundamental Hypothesis

The entire Farsight methodology rests on a specific hypothesis about the nature of human consciousness:

**Humans are composite beings with two fundamental aspects:**
1. **The Physical Body** - connected to the physical realm of solid matter
2. **The Soul (Subspace Aspect)** - existing in what is termed "subspace"

These two aspects are both separate from and connected to each other. The physical realm and subspace are distinct but interfaced dimensions of reality.

### The Subspace Mind

The subspace aspect of consciousness has radically different properties than physical consciousness:

- **Omnipresent across space and time** - it exists everywhere at once
- **Instantaneous perception** - no delay in accessing information
- **Non-local awareness** - not bound by physical location or temporal sequence
- **Intuitive rather than analytical** - processes information differently from the conscious mind

### The Problem: Physical Dominance

While we are composite beings (having both body and soul), physical stimuli tend to dominate our awareness. The five physical senses (taste, touch, sight, hearing, smell) overshadow the more intuitive awareness originating from the subspace side. This means most people are not aware they even have a subspace aspect. In the Farsight framework, "soul voices are deafened by the din of our five physical senses."

### The Solution: Awareness Shifting

Remote viewing does not require:
- Leaving your physical body
- Inducing an altered state of consciousness
- Forcing anything to happen

Instead, remote viewing involves **shifting one's awareness from the physical senses to the subspace aspect**. This happens automatically once a person's awareness is no longer riveted on the physical side of life. The techniques focus on quieting physical sensory input and conscious mind activity, allowing the subspace perception to emerge naturally.

### The Three-Second Window

A crucial discovery in the Farsight research is the **approximately three-second delay** between when the subspace mind obtains information and when the conscious mind can react to it. The SRV protocols are specifically designed to exploit this window, allowing the viewer to record subspace-accessed data before the conscious mind can contaminate it with analysis, imagination, or rationalization.

## 3. Scientific Remote Viewing (SRV) Methodology

### What Remote Viewing Is

Remote viewing is defined as **a controlled and trainable mental process involving psi (or psychic ability) used to transfer perceptual information across time and space.**

In the Farsight model, remote viewing involves **shifting awareness from one place and time to another**. You do not "go" anywhere. You do not travel. You simply redirect your attention from the physical sensory field to the subspace informational field.

### The Nature of Remote Viewing Perception

The remote viewing experience has distinctive characteristics:

**Low-Resolution but Multi-Sensory:** The subspace mind does not transmit bright, high-resolution images like physical vision. Instead, the viewer tends to "feel" the image as much as visualize it. However, this is not inferior to physical perception because **all five senses plus the subspace sense operate during remote viewing**, providing a rich, multidimensional data stream.

**Bilocation:** Approximately halfway through a session, viewers often begin to feel they are in two places at once - physically present in their body and simultaneously present at the target location. At this point, data typically flows very rapidly.

**Individual Focusing:** Each viewer is naturally attracted to certain aspects of any target. For example, a doctor might perceive medical details about people at a target, while a photographer might focus on visual and environmental characteristics. This is why Farsight uses multiple viewers for important projects - each contributes unique perspectives.

### Meditation as Foundation

Dr. Brown strongly recommends combining remote viewing practice with meditation, particularly Transcendental Meditation (TM), though any effective meditation practice can support remote viewing development. The reason is simple: meditation trains the ability to settle the mind and shift awareness away from physical sensory dominance, which is exactly what remote viewing requires.

### Trainability

According to Farsight's extensive teaching experience: **Any person of average or better intelligence can be trained to remote view with considerable accuracy.** In their week-long introductory classes, all or nearly all students have successful remote-viewing experiences, and most sessions conducted after the third day contain obviously target-related material.

Meditators often progress faster because they already have an intuitive sense of subspace and can more easily discriminate between complex target characteristics.

## 4. The Five-Phase Protocol

Scientific Remote Viewing has five distinct phases that follow sequentially during a session. Each phase brings the viewer into either a closer or an altered association with the target.

SRV is performed by **writing on plain white paper with a pen**, creating sketches and symbols that represent aspects of the target. The viewer then **probes these marks with the pen** to sense intuitive ideas. Since the subspace mind perceives all aspects simultaneously, probing a mark focuses attention on the desired aspect.

### Phase 1: Initial Contact

**Purpose:** Establish initial contact with the target and set up the pattern of data acquisition.

**Process:**
- This is the only phase that directly uses the target coordinates
- The viewer draws what is called an "ideogram" - a spontaneous mark made in response to the coordinates
- The ideogram is then decoded to determine primitive descriptive characteristics of the target
- Once initial contact is established, the coordinates are no longer needed

**Key Principle:** The ideogram is drawn without thought or analysis. The pen moves spontaneously in response to the target.

### Phase 2: Sensory Contact

**Purpose:** Increase viewer contact with the target through all five physical senses.

**Process:**
- Information is obtained through hearing, touch, sight, taste, and smell
- Initial magnitude and dimensional information is gathered
- The viewer records brief, fast sensory impressions

**Structure:**
- **Part A:** Descriptive words (colors, temperatures, textures)
- **Part B:** Dimensionality (sizes, shapes, orientation)
- **Part C:** Energetics and movement (still, flowing, vibrating)
- **Part D:** Emotions, intentions, or ambiance

### Phase 3: Sketch

**Purpose:** Create a visual representation of the target.

**Process:**
- The viewer draws 1-3 "flash sketches" - quick, rough visual responses
- These are not meant to be accurate drawings but spontaneous impressions
- The sketches are then probed for additional information

### Phase 4: Detailed Contact

**Purpose:** Allow the subspace mind significant control in solving the remote-viewing problem.

**Process:**
- The subspace mind directs the flow of information to the conscious mind
- Target contact becomes more detailed and specific
- The viewer has minimal conscious control, allowing deeper subspace data to emerge

### Phase 5: Guided Exploration

**Purpose:** Conduct specialized explorations that would be too leading in earlier phases.

**Process:**
- More directive procedures are allowed
- Includes specialized techniques such as:
  - Locational sketches (placing the target in relation to geographic areas)
  - Timeline explorations
  - Perspective shifts
- Can dramatically increase session productivity

**Critical Rule:** There must be **no deviation from the grammar of the protocols**, especially for novices. Any deviation is designed by the conscious mind, and when this happens, the subspace mind loses control of the session and the data become contaminated.

## 5. Target Cue Construction

### The Target Coordinate System

SRV always focuses on a target using **target coordinates** - two randomly generated four-digit numbers (e.g., 1234/5678) assigned to the target. The remote viewer is not told what the numbers represent until after the session is completed.

**The remarkable finding:** The subspace mind instantly knows the target even when given only its coordinate numbers, while the conscious mind remains ignorant.

### Why Specific Cues Matter

Unlike physical observation where the conscious mind can scan, evaluate, and prioritize, the remote viewing experience is more passive. The viewer perceives what is there but has limited evaluative capability. Therefore, **the target cue must be very specific** about what is desired from the subspace mind.

Excessively vague cues (like just a person's name) allow the subspace mind to perceive anything related to that person at any time, making the data too broad and subjective.

### Target Definition Structure

A complete target definition has three components:

#### 1. Viewing Parameters

**Target Range:** Limits the type of information permissible in the session (typically tangibles and intangibles that exist in the target).

**Time Frame:** Specifies when the target exists. This is crucial because the subspace mind can perceive all timelines (past, present, future) equally. The cue requests the subspace mind to locate targets in time frames and realities closely connected to our present.

#### 2. The Essential Cue

The essential cue uses a segmented structure with parts separated by slashes:

**Format:** Primary Cue / First Secondary Cue / Second Secondary Cue (Temporal Identifier)

**Examples:**
- Napoleon Bonaparte / Battle of Waterloo / event (1815)
- John F. Kennedy assassination / event (22 November 1963)
- Nagasaki / nuclear destruction / event (9 August 1945)

**Critical Principle:** The cue must begin with a **known, not a conclusion**. Errors typically result from placing analytical conclusions in the cue itself.

**Bad Example:** "John F. Kennedy assassination / conspiracy"  
This assumes a conspiracy exists. Remote viewing gathers descriptive data; conclusions come during analysis.

**Good Approach:** Cue the event itself, use movement exercises to gather comprehensive data, then analyze for evidence of conspiracy afterward.

#### 3. The Qualifiers

Following the essential cue is a list of qualifiers - clear descriptions of specific things the viewer is supposed to observe and describe. These must address the primary goals of the cue, including instructions to observe activity at the target location.

## 6. Data Types and Session Conditions

Remote viewing data are classified into six types based on three characteristics:
1. Amount of information the viewer has about the target beforehand
2. Whether the viewer works with a monitor
3. How the target is chosen

### Solo Sessions (No Monitor)

**Type 1 Data:** Solo, viewer picks target (front-loaded)
- Viewer knows the target in advance
- Most difficult and least reliable
- Conscious mind can easily contaminate data
- Rarely attempted even by advanced viewers
- Any findings are considered suspect and require corroboration

**Type 2 Data:** Solo, random from predetermined list
- Computer or intermediary supplies only coordinates
- Viewer conducts session blind (without prior knowledge)
- More reliable than Type 1

**Type 3 Data:** Solo, tasker determines target
- Someone else (tasker) determines the target
- Viewer receives only coordinates
- Blind session
- Advanced viewers typically receive no information beyond coordinates

### Monitored Sessions

A **monitor** is a person who navigates the session, telling the viewer what to do, where to look, and where to go. This allows the viewer to disengage analytical mental resources while the monitor handles analysis.

**Type 4 Data:** Monitored, monitor knows target
- Monitor knows the target but communicates only coordinates to viewer
- Often used in training
- Useful for research since monitor has maximum information to direct the viewer
- Problem: Advanced viewers can become so telepathically sensitive they pick up subtle cues from the monitor (breathing changes, grunts, etc.), potentially contaminating data

**Type 5 Data:** Monitored, double-blind
- Neither viewer nor monitor knows the target
- Target comes from outside agency or computer program
- Most reliable for advanced work
- Disadvantage: Monitor cannot sort out most useful information during session
- Solution: Pre-written scripts with clear instructions but no target-identifying information

**Type 6 Data:** Both front-loaded
- Both monitor and viewer know the target
- Rarely collected anymore
- Was occasionally used when few trained viewers existed and information was urgently needed

### Standard Procedure for Human Viewers

The Alliance protocol follows core SRV principles but is adapted for AI-assisted training:

1. **Write Target Coordinates** - establishes the "distraction pattern"
2. **Draw First Ideogram** - spontaneous, no thought or analysis
3. **Probe the Ideogram** - record basic descriptors and impressions
4. **Complete Parts A-D** - brief, fast entries for each category
5. **Draw Flash Sketches** - 1-3 quick, rough sketches
6. **Continue until complete or fatigued**
7. **Upload full session** - complete transcript including drawings must be uploaded to Farsight (summaries are insufficient)

The AI partner serves as monitor with specific responsibilities:

- Provide gentle support and direction
- Identify deductions (conclusions) and mark them as "D-"
- Ask clarifying questions: "What did you actually perceive that led to that conclusion?"
- Encourage descriptive clarity without leading

### Human-Specific Guidelines

**Core Principles:**
- **Do not interpret** - your job is to perceive, not analyze
- **Stay low-level** - avoid conclusions, symbols, or analysis during the session
- **First impression is most accurate** - trust initial perceptions
- **Avoid stating what is NOT there** - only report what you DID perceive

## 8. Practical Guidelines for Remote Viewing

### Essential Principles

**Communication with the Soul:** Subspace information has a mental flavor distinctly different from the five physical senses. It is much more subtle and delicate. For this reason:

- Sensory input from the five physical senses must be kept to minimum before and during sessions
- Begin with meditation or other procedures to calm the mind
- Shift awareness away from physical senses

**The Conscious Mind Problem:** The thinking, judgmental, and evaluative processes of the conscious mind can contaminate accurately perceived information. The amount of information the conscious mind has about the target during the session must be minimized.

**Reading Intuition:** SRV systematizes the reading of intuition - that feeling about something you otherwise would have no direct knowledge of on the physical level. Using SRV, information from the subspace mind is recorded before the conscious mind can interfere with rationalization or imagination.

### The Remote Viewing Experience

**What It Feels Like:** When at peace inwardly and generally stress-free, beginners perceive a target with a quality that tends to "feel" the image as much as visualize it. The human subspace mind does not transmit bright, high-resolution images.

**Not Inferior, Just Different:** The relatively low-resolution remote viewing experience is not inferior to visual experience based on eyesight. All five senses plus the subspace sense operate, making it possible to obtain a much higher-quality collection of diverse and penetrating data.

**Bilocation:** Approximately halfway through a session, the viewer often begins to feel present in two places at once. Data flow becomes very fast at this point.

### Training and Development

**Who Can Learn:** Any person of average or better intelligence can be trained to remote view with considerable accuracy.

**Success Rate:** In Farsight's week-long introductory classes, all or nearly all students have successful experiences. Most sessions after the third day contain obviously target-related material.

**Meditation Advantage:** Meditators often have more penetrating and profound experiences more quickly because they already have a good intuitive sense of subspace.

**Natural vs. Trained Viewers:** Natural psychics typically use no formal means of data acquisition and simply "feel" the target. Their accuracy depends on how well they can do this, but without understanding the mechanism, they often have uneven success. Trained viewers using SRV protocols tend to have more consistent results.

### Critical Success Factors

**No Protocol Deviation:** There must be no deviation from the grammar of the protocols, especially for novices. Deviations are designed by the conscious mind, causing the subspace mind to lose control and contaminating the data.

**Stress-Free State:** Remote viewing works best when the viewer is at peace inwardly and generally stress-free.

**Not Forced:** Remote perception works best when it is not forced in any way. It is a natural process of a deeply settled mind.

**Record Immediately:** Information must be recorded within the three-second window before the conscious mind can react and contaminate it.
